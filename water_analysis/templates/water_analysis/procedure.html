Procedure for Water Analysis

pH Measurement

Apparatus Required: pH meter, beaker

Chemicals Required: Standard buffer solutions (pH 4, 7.01)

Procedure:

  1. Calibrate the pH meter using standard buffer solutions of pH 7.01.
  2. Rinse the electrode with distilled water and blot dry.
  3. Submerge the electrode in the pH 4 and stir gently.
  4. Wait for the reading to stabilize and record the pH value and check if the reading is correct
  5. Submerge the pH meter in the water sample and measure the reading

    6. OR

  6. use pH strips and guess the approximate pH of the water sample.

Turbidity Measurement

Apparatus Required: Transparency Tube, Secchi Disk, Marker, Measuring device

Procedure:

  1. Fill the tube with a water sample and mark the initial water depth.
  2. Look down over the top of the tube while you slowly release the valve at the base.
  3. Continue to release the water until you can barely make out the secchi disk, then record the remaining water depth.
  4. Measure the depth using the Measuring device and note it down

TDS Measurement

Apparatus Required:Beaker, filter paper, Evaporating dish, stirring stick,pippete, weighing scale

Procedure:

  1. Stir the water sample in the beaker with your stirring stick. Stir vigorously enough to agitate the solution
  2. Collect 50 mL of the water in the pipette. Make sure you're still stirring the water while collecting the sample — don't let the solution settle before you pipette your smaller sample.
  3. Put the 50 mL water sample from the pipette through the filter paper three times to ensure all particulate matter has been collected in the filter
  4. Weigh the evaporating dish with the filtrate.
  5. Transfer the filtrate from the previous step to the evaporating dish you weighed in step 2, and wait for the filtrate to dry completely. Once the dish and filtrate are dry, weigh them again

Alkalinity Measurement

Apparatus Required:Conical flask, Burette, stand, pippete, Measuring cylinder

Required Chemicals: 0.02N HCl, Water sample, Phenolphthalein indicator, Methyl orange indicator

Procedure:

  1. Fill the Burette with 0.02N HCl
  2. Take 10ml of water sample in a conical flask.
  3. Add 2 drops of Phenolphthalein indicator (pink color will appear), start titrating till the solution becomes colorless and note the reading
  4. Add 2-3 drops of Methyl orange (yellow color will appear), titrate till the solution turns pinkish orange and note the reading.

Hardness Measurement

Apparatus Required:Conical flask, Burette, stand, Test tuber

Required Chemicals: 0.01M ZnSO4, 0.01M NaEDTA, Water sample, EBT indicator, buffer solution

Procedure:

Part A: Standardization of EDTA solution

  1. Fill the Burette with 0.01M EDTA solution
  2. Get 0.01M ZnSo4 solution in conical flask, put buffer solution in it and stir it.
  3. Add 1-2 drops of EBT indicator (purple color will appear), titrate till color changes from purple to blue and note the reading
  4. Add 2-3 drops of Methyl orange (yellow color will appear), titrate till the solution turns pinkish orange and note the reading.

Part B: Finding total hardness of the water sample

  1. Fill the Burette with EDTA solution
  2. Get 10ml of water sample in a conical flask
  3. Add buffer solution in the conical flask
  4. Add 1 to 2 drops of EBT indicator to the solution (purple color will appear), titrate against the EDTA solution till the color changes from purple to blue and note the reading
